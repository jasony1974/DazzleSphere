Primrose Meets Lacey

Once Upon a time, Primrose lived with her sister, Spangle and Spangle's friends, the Dazzles. Primrose didn't know the Dazzles very well --after all, she had spent the past eight years in England, only returning a few months ago! So, she usually stayed close to Spangle. Until this one time where Primrose was all alone.... without Spangle.

It was a Tuesday, and the weather was pleasantly warm. Primrose was in Dazzle City's Central Park admiring the flowers-- more specifically, the primroses.

Primrose thought: "With my sister away in Dazzle Town, working on her latest project with Francesca and Glossy Dazzle, I get to relax and look at all the flowers I want!"

Primrose paused.

"Official downside. No more Spangle. Official upside-- less Dazzles to run into, heehee."

Primrose looked carefully at the flowers. A couple strolled by. Three kids and a mother walked past. As she continued looking at the flowers she decided that they looked prettier than on Sunday.

"I think you look lovely, little flowers!"" she told them.

Her phone buzzed. Primrose looked at it; it was an app update. As she started to put it away, she had an idea.

"I know! I'll text Spangle a little hello!"

P: Hiya!

S: Hey.

P: Busy?

S: Yep

P: K bye

S: bye

"Well, I guess I'll find out all about it when she gets home."

As she put her phone away, she heard a voice from the other side of the hedge.

"Oh, you sweet little thing. All happy in the sunlight, aren't you? And don't you smell wonderful."

Someone talking to the flowers, like she did! Primrose peeked around the bush and saw a girl about her age. She had a white frilly dress on, but wore a western vest as well. She knew that girl! It was Lacey Dazzle. But did she want to talk to a Dazzle right now?

"And look at you, beautiful! I do believe you are looking more radiant than the last time I saw you."

Primrose couldn't help but chuckle. Lacey gasped, and looked over her shoulder at Primrose.

"Oh! I didn't see you there. You caught me talking to the flowers."

Primrose leaned over and took a big sniff of the flower Lacey had talked to.

"Just like me." Primrose told her.

Lacey looked shy.

"Really?"

Primrose nodded. Then she looked back to the flowers and reached out to touch one gently.

"Your petals look lovely today, Ella!"

Lacey's eyebrows went up.

"You name the flowers too?"

Primrose nodded again.

"There's a bellflower over there that I named Glissen because it looked so happy, bouncing in the breeze."

Lacey giggled.

"I'd like to call that Peony Francesca, it looks so elegant."

Primrose smiled.

"I was going to look at the Oriental poppies, would you like to come?"

"Of course."

As they walked, Primrose asked , "Which flowers do you fancy?"

Lacey looked puzzled. "Fancy?"" she repeated.

"Oh, sorry." Primrose tried again, "Do you have a favorite flower?"

"Oh. Blush pink roses. You?"

"Primroses, of course!"" Primrose laughed.

"And for fragrance? What flower do you —" Lacey paused, "er, fancy?"

"Freesia. You?"

"Lily of the Valley, always and forever."" Lacey was quite certain.

"They do smell rather glorious."" Primrose agreed.

"Ooo, the irises are in bloom, look at that color!""

LATER....

The girls got ice cream together and sat on a white bench by a hedge of brightly colored flowers to eat it.

"It's my dream to visit the botanical gardens in Shanghai." Primrose licked her strawberry cone.

"Me too! And go to the Holland Tulip festival."" Lacey sighed. "Wouldn't it be lovely to travel the world just to see the flowers in every country?"

"Yes, it would! Although you are the first person I've found that thinks so Lacey!"

"Yeah, Glissen would be so bored, right?"

"She is rather...lively."

Lacey snorted into her ice cream. "Definitely. Sometimes she can be too much for me."

Primrose looked at her.

"I forgot. Living with all of your sisters must be quite a different experience for you."

"Yeah. Before, it was... A lot quieter. Most of the time."

"Are you..." Primrose paused. "Um, afraid your — I don't know what to call her..."

"Step-mom," Lacey helped Primrose out. Talking about her past was so awkward! "I used to call her Mom... I mean, she was the one who raised me. Sort of."

"Are you afraid she'll find you?"" Primrose asked.

"Nah. She wasn't ever mean to me...and she was hardly ever there." After a moment, Lacey crunched up the rest of her sugar cone, and said, "It's nice to have family around, to feel a part of something. And even though it's a little unusual to have someone always looking out for me, I'm getting used to it. I like it. Hey, that reminds me; I wanted to bring dessert home for everyone. What do you think? Another round of ice cream for later?"

"Ice cream twice in one day?" Primrose grinned. "Brilliant! Or, best day ever!"

"I like brilliant!" Lacey smiled. "I might start saying that."

On their way home, Primrose's phone rang. "Hey, it's Spangle. She must have time to talk. Hey sis," she said into the phone.

"Hey, how's it going? Sorry about before; I was in the middle of a "this isn't the door we ordered" crisis. Are you okay?" Spangle sounded concerned.

"Yes, I'm fine. Didn't mean to worry you! Is the door problem all sorted now?"

"Yeah, it was just a mix-up. I'll tell you all about it when I get home — which will be a day or so later than I expected. I was gonna call sooner to tell you, but..."

"The door happened."" Primrose said.

"Yup. Anyway, I'm really sorry. Are you going to be alright?" Spangle asked anxiously.

Primrose looked over at Lacey, who was looking happily at the flavors of ice cream she carried.

"Yes, Spangle. I am good. Actually, I'm great." She smiled. "I'll tell you all about it when you come home. No worries."

LATER...

Lacey and Primrose got home later that evening. So did Francesca!

"Hi, girls!" Francesca smiled.

"Hi," Lacey and Primrose waved.

Spangle and Glossy are going to finish the building later this week, then I'll work on the inside for a few days," Francesca informed them.

"YAY!" Primrose cried, "That's the quickest Spangle's ever done when building!"

"Good for you!" Lacey smiled.

"Say, Lacey?" said Primrose, "Let's promise to be B.F.F.F.S!"

"Best flower friends forever!" Lacey smiled.

And the girls pinky promised to be best friends for forever and ever and ever.

THE END!
